# Pitch Architecture (12-Step Debate-Case Framework)

Every strong piece of communication follows this structure. Maps to Public Forum debate: Claim > Warrant > Evidence > Impact.

## The 12 Steps

1. **Bold Hook** - Absolute claim. Demands credibility. No warmup. Forces the reader to agree or push back.
2. **Define the Enemy** - Name the immediate problem, not the category. "Teams using AI are stepping on each other" not "AI coordination is a challenge."
3. **Real Numbers** - Specific and citable. If you can't cite it, it's weak.
4. **Macro Zoom** - Scale the stakes. Zoom out from specific to systemic.
5. **Personal Pivot** - "I decided to fix that." Conviction bridge. Unarguable.
6. **Solution Specifics** - Concrete and measurable. Not "we solve coordination."
7. **Ethos** - Background that qualifies YOU specifically. Specific fit, not generic credibility.
8. **Pattern Insight** - The lesson experience taught you. Shows judgment, not just history.
9. **Values via Sequencing** - The ORDER you say things communicates your philosophy. Show don't tell.
10. **Future Prediction** - "Here's what's coming that most people don't see yet."
11. **Crystallizing One-Liner** - One sentence that makes the whole thing inevitable.
12. **CTA with Pre-Solved Friction** - Predict the objection before it lands. Defuse it in the ask.

## Rules
- Never START with the solution. Start with the enemy (step 2) or the hook (step 1).
- Never state values directly. Sequence evidence to imply them.
- The one-liner is not a tagline. It's a logical conclusion the reader arrives at.
- Pre-solve friction BEFORE the CTA.
- Real numbers or "unknown." Never fabricate a stat.

## Debate Mechanics
- Claim: The assertion (bold, declarative, no hedging)
- Warrant: The logical reason the claim is true
- Evidence: The citable proof
- Impact: Why it matters at scale

Every strong paragraph has all four. If one is missing, the paragraph is weak.
