# Viral Board: Trending Topics and Proven Formats

## FORMATS THAT WORK (Steal These)

### Format 1: The Anti-Hormozi Talking Head
One person, direct to camera, bold statement, dynamic text overlays. Same structure as Hormozi but LESS energy. Calm, deadpan, dry. That's the differentiator.

### Format 2: The Screen Recording Demo
Raw screen recording of product working, with voiceover. The demo IS the content. No editing needed. "So this just happened."

### Format 3: The "Mistake Call-Out"
"3 mistakes every founder makes with AI voice." Triggers self-checking. Deadpan delivery. Don't lecture. State the mistake and move on.

### Format 4: The Contrarian / Bold Claim
Open with a statement so strong the viewer needs to see if you're right or insane. "Voice AI will kill 60% of SaaS dashboards." Pause. "Here's the math."

### Format 5: The "Nobody's Talking About This"
Position as insider with exclusive knowledge. FOMO + curiosity.

### Format 6: The Day-in-the-Life
Behind-the-scenes of building the company. Raw, not polished. Authenticity outperforms polish by 31%.

### Format 7: The Duet/Stitch React
React to someone else's video. Piggybacks on viral content. Give your take in 15 seconds. Deadpan.

## CONTENT ANGLES THAT WORK
- "Agentic AI" buzzword: "Everyone's talking about it. Nobody can explain it."
- "Voice Is the Next Interface": react and add what's missing
- "Boring AI Business" counter-narrative: boring wins, real problems
- AI Agent Product Demos: 30-second screen recording with dry commentary
- "AI Replacing Jobs" discourse: nuanced take, not fear-mongering
- OODA Loop in business: military frameworks for startup decisions

## INSPIRATION PROCESS
When you see something interesting:
1. What's the core insight? (One sentence)
2. What's the Walker angle? (How does this connect to MEOP?)
3. What's the hook? (First 3 seconds)
4. What format? (Talking head, screen recording, react, day-in-the-life?)
5. Film it. Don't overthink.
