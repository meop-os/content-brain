# Guerrilla Marketing Ideas

## S-TIER (Film These First)

### 1. "Hacking Our Own Client" (Filmed Pentest)
With client permission, film a real pentest. Show the defense catching and killing the attack in real-time.
Hook: "We're about to hack our own client" > attack sequence > defense fires > "That's what $X/month buys you."

### 2. "The Computer That Fights Back"
Griffin tries to hack Walker's laptop on camera. Laptop detects it and goes insane. Red alerts, warnings, processes killing themselves. Griffin's terminal: ACCESS DENIED. Walker, calm: "It does that."

### 3. "We Left a Laptop in a Coffee Shop"
Leave a protected laptop in public with hidden camera. Film what happens when someone tries to access it. Laptop locks, alarm triggers. Walker returns, opens with one touch. "We're good."

## A-TIER (High Impact)

### 4. "Live Hack Challenge" ($10K Bounty)
Livestream bounty challenge. $10K if anyone breaches the system. Film attempts failing live.

### 5. "Your Password Is..." (Street Interviews)
Approach people. Show them how exposed their info is. Offer to show protection.

### 6. "AI vs. AI" Showdown
Offensive AI vs defensive system. Split-screen. Narrate like a boxing match.

### 7. "How Fast Can We Hack [Industry]?"
Series: pick an industry each episode. Show how fast a typical company could be compromised. Then show the fix.

## B-TIER (Supporting)

### 8. "The Kill Switch" Demo
Show the kill switch in action. Press it. Total system destruction in real-time. Dramatic.

### 9. "Founder vs. Founder" CTF
Walker and Griffin capture-the-flag. Both perspectives.

### 10. "We Scanned Your Company" (Cold Outreach)
Run free security scan on prospect. Send results. Film their reaction.

### 11. "24 Hours of Attacks" Time-lapse
Honeypot recording every attack over 24 hours. Beautiful data visualization.

### 12. "What Hackers See" (POV Content)
First-person breach attempt. Then flip to defender's screen.

## Framework
1. Demo IS the content
2. Simple enough for your mom
3. Real, not scripted
4. Visual spectacle (make cybersecurity VISIBLE)
5. Stakes (money, reputation, or genuine danger)
