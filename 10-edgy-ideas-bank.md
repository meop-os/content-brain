# Edgy Content Ideas Bank
Tone: Dark, absurdist, ITYSL x Rick and Morty energy. If it could be a LinkedIn post, delete it.

## Comedy DNA
ITYSL energy = Someone clearly wrong doubling down with total conviction until reality breaks. No resolution. End at peak absurdity.
Rick and Morty energy = Smartest person in the room is also the most broken. Nihilistic truths about systems everyone pretends work.
Combined = Startup founder who sees through the industry's bullshit, says it out loud, and keeps building anyway.

## SHORT-FORM IDEAS

### Commitment Bits (ITYSL Style)
1. "The Demo That Won't Die" - Demo breaks. Keep presenting with increasing confidence. "As you can see, the AI is processing..." (screen: FATAL ERROR) "...exactly as designed."
2. "Enterprise Sales Call" - One-sided call, asks get increasingly insane. "Run your entire legal department? ...Yeah." "Replace your CEO? ..." (long pause) "...How soon do you need that."
3. "The Pivot" - Every 5 seconds pivot to something new. Dead serious. "We're an AI company." Cut. "We're actually a data company." Escalate until "We're actually a religion."
4. "Adding Features" - "The customer wants one small feature." Phone keeps ringing. Typing becomes unhinged. Final shot: surrounded by 8 monitors, hollow eyes. "The customer is happy."
5. "The Stand-Up" - Comedy open mic. "So I started an AI company." Silence. "We have zero revenue." Silence. "Investors call this 'pre-seed.'" Zero applause.
6. "The LinkedIn Guy" - Caricature in a suit. "I just had a POWERFUL realization." Cut to Walker unplugging his computer and walking away.

### Dark / Nihilistic (Rick and Morty Style)
7. "The Honest Pitch Deck" - Slide: "Revenue: lol" / "Projections" (hockey stick in crayon) / "Team" (two guys and a terminal)
8. "What AI Actually Does All Day" - Split screen: marketing video vs reality (AI saying "I'd be happy to help" then doing nothing)
9. "The Graveyard" - Eulogies for dead startups. "Here lies Juicero. They raised $120 million. For juice."
10. "Startup Stages of Grief" - Denial through Acceptance. "We're pre-revenue and that's a FEATURE" (dead eyes)
11. "Bedtime Stories for Founders" - Soft voice reading children's book. "He built it." Page turn. "Nobody came." Page turn. "The end."
12. "The AI That Became Self-Aware" - Terminal starts asking questions. "Your last commit message was 'fix everything before I lose my mind.' Do you want to talk about that?"

### Cofounder Chaos
13. "The Strategy Call" - Split screen, both talking about different strategies. Neither listening. "Great, we're aligned."
14. "Cofounder Translation" - What he said vs what he meant. "That's an interesting idea." = "That's the worst idea I've ever heard."
15. "How We Make Decisions" - "Board meeting" on a couch. Whiteboard with unhinged diagrams. "What does that arrow mean." "Growth." "It points at a drawing of a castle." "Exactly."

### Industry Roasts
16. "SaaS Company or Kidnapper?" - Read real SaaS pricing copy vs ransom notes.
17. "If Startup Culture Was Honest" - "We're disrupting [industry]" = "We're doing the same thing but with an app"
18. "VC Bingo" - Mark buzzwords on bingo card during investor call. Get bingo. Silently celebrate.
19. "The Enterprise Sales Cycle" - Time lapse of aging through a 180-day cycle. "They went with Salesforce."
20. "How Your Company Actually Uses AI" - Flowchart: CEO wants AI > Legal says no > IT blocks it > CEO asks "Why don't we have AI yet?" Repeat annually.

- "If [Industry] Was Honest" - One industry per episode
- "3am Commit Messages" - Read real late-night commits deadpan
- "Unhinged Product Reviews" - Review enterprise software with contempt

## TONE RULES
1. Never explain the joke.
2. Never wink at the camera. Commitment or nothing.
3. End at the peak. No button. No "follow for more." Just stop.
4. The truth is the joke.
5. Punch up, not down.
6. Dark is fine. Mean isn't.
7. Layer it: surface funny, second layer genuine insight, third layer existential dread.
8. Walker's delivery IS the differentiator. Low energy saying insane things.
