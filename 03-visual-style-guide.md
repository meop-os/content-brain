# Meep Visual Style Guide
> Derived from Walker's reference images. Updated: 2026-03-26.
> Add new references to `social-styles/` folder. Meep reads them on every session.

---

## 5 Visual Formats

### 1. Tweet Screenshot Card
**When:** Authority/quote posts. Sharing someone else's insight or your own hot take framed as a tweet.
**Style:** White background, profile pic + name + handle at top, large readable text, play button overlay optional.
**Reference:** Hormozi tweet screenshots (18.30.10, 18.32.16)
**Key details:** Profile badge (verified check), clean sans-serif font, generous padding, mobile-screenshot crop ratio (vertical).

### 2. Clean Text on Paper
**When:** Wisdom posts. Timeless insights. Reframing a common belief.
**Style:** Off-white/cream textured paper background. Serif or clean sans-serif font. Black text. No images. One powerful idea.
**Reference:** "That expert who fixed your problem" (18.35.33), "Becoming the best version" with illustration (18.35.58)
**Key details:** Large font size (readable on phone), generous margins, sometimes paired with a simple pen/ink illustration. Feels analog, not digital.

### 3. Bold Carousel Cover
**When:** List posts, educational carousels, "X reasons why" content.
**Style:** Cream/paper background, bold black sans-serif headline, one accent color highlight (green, cyan), arrow icon indicating swipe.
**Reference:** "15 Reasons" (18.36.53), "The top 1% share these 8 traits" (18.37.15)
**Key details:** Mixed font weights (bold + regular), highlighted keyword in accent color, minimal — text only on cover slide. Arrow signals more content.

### 4. Infographic / Data Wheel
**When:** Framework posts, "X skills/traits/steps" educational content.
**Style:** White background, central concept in colored circle, spokes to labeled sections with bullet points, small icons per section.
**Reference:** "9 Soft Skills" wheel (18.38.29)
**Key details:** Colorful but not cluttered. Each section has 3-4 bullet points. Numbered. Icons are simple line-art. Readable at phone size.

### 5. News Photo + Caption
**When:** Breaking news, current events, industry news.
**Style:** Real photo of the person/company, bold white or black text overlay at bottom, optional circular inset image showing context.
**Reference:** Bezos manufacturing/AI (18.39.32)
**Key details:** Photo takes 60-70% of frame, text is bold sans-serif at bottom, reads like a news headline. Optional small context image in circle overlay.

## Universal Rules

- **Vertical orientation** for all social visuals (1080x1350 or 1080x1920)
- **Readable at phone size** — if you have to zoom, the text is too small
- **One idea per image** — never cram two concepts
- **No stock photo energy** — real photos, illustrations, or pure text. Never generic corporate imagery.
- **No Meop branding on visuals** unless specifically requested — the content should feel organic, not branded
- **Brand colors available** but used sparingly: #0a0a0f (black), #00ff41 (neon green), #00d4ff (cyan)

---

## What NOT to Make

- Generic "data cards" with dark backgrounds and neon stats (AI slop)
- Gradient-heavy corporate graphics
- Stock photo + text overlay (LinkedIn cringe)
- Busy infographics with 15+ data points
- Anything that looks like Canva's default templates
