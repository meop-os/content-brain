# TikTok / Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts Guide

## Platform Specs
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical)
- Resolution: 1080x1920
- Video length sweet spot: 15-45 seconds for virality
- Caption limit: 4,000 characters
- Hashtags: 3-5 targeted
- Sound: Critical on TikTok, less so on Reels
- Posting frequency: 1-3x/day for growth
- Best times: 7-9am, 12-3pm, 7-11pm

## Our Formula
HOOK (0-2s) > TENSION/SETUP (2-10s) > PAYOFF (10-20s) > CTA (last 3s)

## What Works
- Relatable founder content (pain, struggle, wins)
- Comedy sketches (startup life, AI absurdity, business humor)
- Quick educational hits ("here's what nobody tells you about...")
- Trending audio adaptations (take trends, twist to startup/AI)
- Behind the scenes (real, unpolished, authentic)
- Series content (people come back for recurring formats)

## What Doesn't Work
- Corporate content (instant scroll)
- Polished ads (algorithm buries them)
- Long explanations without entertainment
- Content that looks like it took too long to make

## Comedy Formats

### 1. THE SKETCH (15-45s)
Setup > Build > Punchline. Relatable business pain.

### 2. THE POV (10-30s)
"POV: [situation]" Camera is the audience's eyes.

### 3. THE SKIT SERIES (30-60s)
Recurring characters, recurring format. Algorithm loves series.

### 4. THE REACTION (15-45s)
React to something ridiculous/real. Hot takes, industry news.

### 5. THE "DID YOU KNOW" (15-30s)
Quick educational hit wrapped in entertainment.

### 6. THE DAY IN THE LIFE (45-90s)
Exaggerated or real slice of founder life.

## Caption Rules
- First line IS the hook (shows before "see more")
- Use line breaks generously
- Hashtags at end: 1 broad + 2 niche + 1-2 trending
- Tone: casual, punchy, direct
- End with a question or challenge to drive comments

## Instagram Specifics
- Reels can repurpose TikTok (remove TikTok watermark)
- Saves are IG's favorite metric. Create saveable content.
- Carousels outperform single images for education
- Stories: 3-7/day with interactive elements (polls, questions)
- Feed post dimensions: 1080x1350 portrait (takes more screen space)
