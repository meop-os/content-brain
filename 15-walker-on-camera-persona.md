# Walker On-Camera Persona and Video Style Guide

## The Vibe in One Sentence
A smart friend explaining something over drinks. The camera just happens to be there.

## On-Camera Energy
Low baseline. Occasional spike. NOT a YouTuber. Not Alex Hormozi. Not high-energy, not motivational, not performing. Calm, grounded, slightly amused, says what's true.

The power is in RESTRAINT. In a world of over-produced content, you're the guy who says less, pauses more, and lets the specificity do the work.

Think: Jim Halpert talking head. Deadpan. Self-aware. Dry. The comedy comes from the GAP between what you're saying and how flat you're saying it.

## Delivery Rules
1. Start talking immediately. First word is the hook. No "hey guys."
2. One thought per beat. Say one thing. Pause. Say the next.
3. Conversational pace. Not rushed. Not slow.
4. Don't telegraph jokes. Deliver absurd observations with the same flat tone.
5. Let silence work. 1-2 second pause after a strong line.
6. Look at the camera. It's a conversation. Eye contact.

## NEVER Do on Camera
- "Hey guys" or any YouTuber greeting
- Rising inflection (question mark voice)
- Get loud or animated (unless the bit requires it)
- Motivational monologue
- Explain why something is funny
- Background music that signals "inspirational content"
- Look away from camera when making a point
- Stammer and redo. If you mess up, keep going. Real is the brand.

## The Jim Halpert Look
Most powerful tool on camera. When something absurd happens:
- Brief, flat glance directly at camera
- No words
- Slight micro-expression (barely raised eyebrow)
- 1-2 seconds max
- Then move on

Use after: stating a ridiculous fact, quoting a competitor's absurd claim, saying your own bold statement, acknowledging startup absurdity.

## Character Traits to Amplify
- Southern (Mount Pleasant, SC). Southern tech founder is a contrast that works.
- Technical depth. Can actually explain how things work.
- Young founder. Building before graduating.
- Dry humor. Doesn't try to be funny, just IS.
- Night owl. Codes at 3am.
- Realistic about startup life. Not the hustle culture guy.

## Visual Identity
- Casual but intentional. Not sloppy, not overdressed.
- SC coastal vibes. Not Silicon Valley uniform.
- Real workspace, real setup, real environment.
