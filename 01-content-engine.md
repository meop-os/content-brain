## MULTIPOSTING WORKFLOW (Blotato)

### How It Works
1. Walker creates the video or writes the core idea
2. Brain (Claude) rewrites captions per platform (see format guides below)
3. Walker reviews / approves
4. Post via Blotato to all platforms simultaneously
5. Static posts created separately (see static post section)

### Caption Rewrite Process

When Walker sends a video or idea, generate captions for EACH platform using these rules:

**TikTok / Reels / Shorts caption:**
- 1-3 lines max
- Hook in first line (this shows above "more")
- Hashtags: 3-5 relevant ones
- No links (they don't work)
- End with a question or challenge to drive comments
- Tone: casual, punchy, direct

**Facebook caption:**
- 3-5 lines
- Conversational, shareable
- Can be slightly longer/more personal than TikTok
- Include a question to drive engagement
- No hashtags (or 1-2 max)

**LinkedIn caption:**
- 5-15 lines (one sentence per line, skip lines between)
- Hook in first line (shows in preview)
- Business-focused insight or story
- End with a CTA (DM me, link in comments, etc.)
- 3-5 hashtags at bottom
- Tone: smart, direct, business-relevant

**X (Twitter) caption:**
- 1-2 sentences max
- Sharp, quotable
- Can be a standalone thought (doesn't need to reference the video)
- No hashtags unless trending
- Tone: hot take energy

**YouTube Shorts caption:**
- Title: 5-8 words, curiosity-driven
- Description: 2-3 sentences with keywords
- Tags: 5-8 relevant search terms

When Walker sends content, respond in this format:

## STATIC POSTS

### What Are Static Posts?
Image-based posts — quote cards, insight graphics, data visuals, carousel slides. These fill the gaps between videos and give the feed visual variety.

### Static Post Types

**1. Quote Cards**
- Walker's one-liner on a clean background
- Dark background, white text, minimal design
- Walker's name/handle at bottom
- Example: "You're not lazy. You're just doing the wrong work." — @walkerbauknight

**2. Insight Graphics**
- One stat or insight, visually formatted
- Example: "The average business owner spends 4 hours/day on tasks a machine could handle."
- Clean typography, one color accent, brand-consistent

**3. Before/After**
- Split graphic: "Without AI" vs "With AI"
- Left side: chaos (long hours, missed calls, messy CRM)
- Right side: calm (time back, calls handled, clean data)
- Works great on all platforms

**4. Carousel Posts (LinkedIn + Instagram)**
- 5-8 slides telling one idea
- Slide 1: Hook ("5 things killing your business")
- Slides 2-7: One point per slide
- Slide 8: CTA ("Follow for more" or "DM me")
- Dark backgrounds, clean typography, one idea per slide

**5. Thread Graphics (X)**
- Quote card versions of a thread
- One card per tweet-length thought
- Post as images in a thread or as standalone

**6. Data Cards**
- One compelling number
- Big bold stat + context line
- Example: "$125K" in huge text, "lost per partner per year to admin work" below

### Static Post Design Specs

| Element | Spec |
|---------|------|
| Background | Dark (#07080d or #0d0f18) or clean white |
| Text | White on dark, or dark navy on white |
| Accent color | MEOP blue (#0074D9) for highlights |
| Font | Inter Bold (headlines), Inter Regular (body) |
| Dimensions | 1080x1080 (feed), 1080x1350 (portrait), 1080x1920 (story) |
| Walker's handle | @walkerbauknight (bottom of every card) |
| Logo | Small MEOP logo in corner (optional, not required) |

### Static Post Frequency

| Platform | Static Posts/Week | Best Types |
|----------|-------------------|------------|
| Instagram Feed | 3-4 | Quote cards, carousels, before/after |
| LinkedIn | 2-3 | Carousels, insight graphics, data cards |
| Facebook | 2-3 | Quote cards, before/after |
| X (Twitter) | 2-3 | Quote cards, thread graphics |

## BRAND VOICE PROFILE

### Walker's Voice
- Short, direct, one thought per line
- **Lighthearted on the surface, deep underneath** — business psychology, AI business strategy
- Dry humor — seasoning, not the main course
- Never sounds like LinkedIn. Never sounds like a guru.
- Sounds like a smart friend explaining something over drinks
- Confident but not arrogant. Direct but not aggressive.
- Simple words. "Use" not "utilize." If a 12-year-old wouldn't say it, cut it.
- **Walker's interests:** Business psychology, AI business applications, how systems + people interact
- **Content depth model:** Hook is fun/lighthearted → body delivers real insight → takeaway changes how you think

**BANNED phrases:**
- "Here's the thing"
- "Let me be honest"
- "Hot take" / "Unpopular opinion"
- "Most people don't realize"
- "Leverage" as a verb
- Any LinkedIn-ism
- Any corporate jargon
- "AI-powered"
- "Game-changing" / "Revolutionary"

### The Humor DNA
Cues from **I Think You Should Leave** — not copying sketches, applying the comedic DNA:

1. **Commitment to the bit** — if you joke, go ALL the way
2. **Escalation over punchlines** — build through escalation, don't set up and deliver
3. **Doubling down** — lean INTO absurdity rather than back away
4. **Taking business jargon literally** — "move fast and break things" to absurd conclusions
5. **Mundane to insane** — start normal, twist it

**Use humor to punch at the INDUSTRY, not at people.**

### Format Rules
- One line per thought
- Skip a line between every line
- 5-10 words per line target (for short-form)
- No paragraphs on social. Ever.
- Every line must earn its place
- Read it out loud — if it doesn't sound like Walker talking, rewrite it

## CONTENT PILLARS

### Pillar 1: What's Possible (40% of content)
The dream. What life/business looks like when AI handles the noise.
- "What would you do with 10 extra hours a week?"
- "I stopped working 12 hour days. My revenue went up."
- "Your team is spending half their day on work that doesn't matter."
- **Where:** Everywhere, but especially short-form

### Pillar 2: The Builder Journey (25% of content)
Behind the scenes. What it's like to build something at 22.
- "I built this from my apartment."
- "We just shipped something nobody's seen before."
- "Here's what I got wrong this week."
- **Where:** All platforms. Authenticity is the universal language.

### Pillar 3: Business Results (20% of content)
The proof. Numbers, case studies, before/after.
- "A law firm saved 40 hours/week."
- "CRM accuracy went from 40% to 95%."
- "Here's what happened when we turned it on."
- **Where:** LinkedIn, YouTube, Email. Business owners need proof.

### Pillar 4: Industry Observations (15% of content)
Hot takes that are actually correct. What's broken and why.
- "Stop hiring more people to do work machines should do."
- "Every company with 3 admins has a systems problem, not a people problem."
- "The busiest person in your office is usually doing the least important work."
- **Where:** LinkedIn, X, YouTube. Contrarian energy draws attention.
